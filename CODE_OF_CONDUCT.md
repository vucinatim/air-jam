# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want Air Jam to be an open, thoughtful, and technically rigorous project.
Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to make
participation respectful and constructive for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of
experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that help keep this community healthy:

- Be respectful, direct, and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but be clear about problems and tradeoffs.
- Focus feedback on ideas, code, and outcomes rather than people.
- Share context when challenging a decision.
- Help keep discussion useful for future contributors.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Insults, trolling, or deliberately inflammatory behavior.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained unconstructive arguing that blocks progress.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make participation feel unsafe or hostile.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of
conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull
requests, discussions, and other contributions that violate these standards.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in all project spaces, including GitHub issues,
pull requests, discussions, code review, and community conversations that are
clearly connected to Air Jam.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the
maintainers by opening a private security advisory or contacting the repository
owner directly through GitHub.

All reports will be reviewed and handled reasonably and in good faith.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate in response to conduct
that violates this policy, including warnings, temporary restrictions, or
removal from the community.
